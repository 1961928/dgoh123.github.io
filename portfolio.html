Week 8

Argument: Public economic data should be made easier to interpret as many people have shown not to understand financial terminology such as inflation.

Conclusion: The data on inflation shows that although some countries show a very high percentage of people who understand the word inflation such as Hongkong or Austria, some countries such as Italy and Romania show that only around 65% of people knew what inflation was. This data supports the argument that this sort of information should make it easier for the general public to understand. Next I look at the Financial literacy score, which is calculated out of 21 using several factors. The data shows some countries such as Colombia, Italy and Romania had a score of 11 out of 21 meaning that a significant amount of people may not understand important economic data released by the government. This again supports the claim that economic data should be made easier to understand for the public.
